Our Mission

AI is making life-altering
decisions. Nobody's watching.

Algorithms decide who gets bail, who gets flagged at the border, and who gets a job interview. Most of the time, no one — not even the judge — knows the system is biased. We change that.

🔍

We find the bias

We audit publicly documented AI tools used by courts, immigration agencies, and corporations — analyzing their accuracy data, demographic disparities, and legal vulnerabilities.

📄

We publish the evidence

Our research reports are free, public, and written to be understood by anyone — not just lawyers. We cite everything. We name names.

🏛️

We advocate for change

We submit public comments on AI legislation, meet with state legislators, and partner with civil liberties organizations to turn our research into policy reform.

📢

We educate communities

Through free Know Your AI Rights workshops, we teach affected communities — immigrants, defendants, job seekers — how algorithms affect their lives and what they can do about it.

What We Do

Research. Expose. Advocate.

Three pillars, one mission — ensuring that the algorithms shaping people's lives are transparent, accurate, and legally accountable.

01

Bias Audits

We analyze AI tools like COMPAS — the sentencing algorithm used in 20+ states — publishing original research on racial disparities, accuracy failures, and due process violations.

02

Policy Advocacy

We submit formal public comments on state and federal AI legislation, request meetings with legislators, and publish quarterly legislative trackers on algorithmic accountability bills.

03

FOIA Investigations

We file Freedom of Information Act requests to obtain government contracts, accuracy data, and demographic breakdowns of AI systems that agencies refuse to disclose voluntarily.

04

Community Workshops

Free 90-minute Know Your AI Rights workshops for affected communities — teaching people how algorithmic tools affect bail, visas, and hiring, and what legal options exist.

05

Corporate Investigations

We investigate the companies selling biased AI to governments — analyzing their lobbying, contracts, and the gap between their marketing claims and actual accuracy records.

06

Chapter Network

We're building a statewide network of student chapters doing this research in their own communities — multiplying our reach and creating the next generation of algorithmic accountability advocates.

Focus Areas

Where algorithms
cause the most harm.

We concentrate our research where algorithmic bias has the most severe, documented impact on civil rights.

⚖️

Criminal Justice

Risk assessment tools like COMPAS influence bail, sentencing, and parole — with documented racial disparities that violate due process. We audit these tools and demand disclosure requirements.

🌍

Immigration

ICE and USCIS use algorithmic risk-scoring to flag individuals for enforcement. These systems operate with almost no outside oversight. We investigate them through FOIA and independent analysis.

💼

Corporate Hiring

Video interview AI tools screen millions of applicants using facial analysis and voice patterns — with evidence of bias against women, people of color, and disabled applicants.

About Us

Built by a student who got tired of waiting for adults to fix it.

"The people most harmed by algorithmic bias are the least likely to know it's happening."

The Algorithmic Justice Project was founded in 2026 by a Michigan high school student with a simple conviction: that serious policy research doesn't require a law degree, and that the communities most affected by AI bias deserve advocates who speak their language.

We are a nonprofit incorporated in Michigan. Our research is independent, our reports are free, and our advocacy is driven entirely by evidence.
